Questions ## Problems ## (aka, *Why yet another framework?*) # Teaching is hard Pre-requisites of modern frameworks make it harder. ### Tech stack pre-reqs - Most suggested setups are "all or nothing" (no "ramp-up") - Results in ubiquity of frameworks with easy ramp-up (e.g. jQuery, PHP) ### Skill pre-reqs - Most are CLI-focused and require compilation for development - Results in requiring JS and CLI skill pre-reqs for HTML changes ### Changing pre-reqs - Many frontend frameworks frequently switch syntaxes (JSX, TS, ES5), API changes, and terminology (e.g. especially jargon around async and state) - Results in fractured docs / tutorials and less transferable skills # Frontend is hard Many popular frontend frameworks can be hard to use in certain situations. ### Dependencies galore - Heavy `node_modules`, and resource-intensive builds - Results in slower dev, un-auditable code, launch disasters (e.g. `left-pad`) ### Difficulty with integration into backend apps - Hybrid apps are clunky to develop - Results in fragile mounting and build systems, perpetual SPA rewrites ### Difficulty in project and team management - "Mere mortals" on-boarding problems with complex FE tech - Results in hard-to-fill roles and poor communication ## Observation: Both are hard for overlapping reasons ## Solution?
